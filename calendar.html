Progress & Meetings

School Reports

When reports are issued and how the assessment cycle works — depending on your child's programme.

ESG · VP · Accu

The school year is structured in three trimesters. Report cards are issued in:

  • December
  • March / April
  • July

Class councils take place before the Christmas, Easter, and Summer holidays to review each student's progress.

ESE — European Section

ESE students receive two types of reports each semester:

  • Midterm reports — an informal overview of progress in each subject, including short teacher comments, issued halfway through the semester.
  • End-of-semester reports — official reports with subject marks (A, B, C) and written comments from teachers and the Class Council.

These reports help track academic performance and support ongoing learning.

Progress & Meetings

Parent-Teacher Speak Date

Thursday, 8 January 2026 — Focused subject-related meetings by appointment.

Short, structured one-on-one conversations with up to four subject teachers, focused on the student's progress in specific subjects.

  • Registration in advance via an online form; preferred time slots can be indicated.
  • Each meeting lasts a maximum of 10 minutes per teacher.
  • The class teacher attends only in their role as subject teacher.

Please note

General topics related to class dynamics or the school community are not discussed during this event.
