Contacts

Key Contacts at LLIS

Most questions follow a simple escalation path. Start with the most direct contact, and only move further up if the matter isn't resolved.

Step 1

Subject Teacher — Academic & Subject Questions

Your first point of contact for any academic or behavioural questions is your child's subject teacher. For questions about lesson content, entries in WebUntis, or general concerns related to a specific subject, reach out by email.

All teachers in the Luxembourg school system can be contacted via a standardised email format:

firstname.lastname@education.lu

If you are unsure about a teacher's name or email address, you can use their IAM code, visible in WebUntis. For example, if the IAM code is SCHFR321, send your email to: SCHFR321@365.education.lu — it will be delivered directly to that teacher.

While email is convenient, a phone call or face-to-face conversation is often preferable — especially for topics that require more nuance or discussion. Please do not use your child's Teams account or WebUntis to contact teachers.

Step 2

Class Teacher — Overall Progress & Class Matters

If your question goes beyond a single subject or concerns your child's overall progress or class behaviour, your next contact is the class teacher. Their email follows the same format as subject teachers.

Class teachers have a broader overview of the learning environment, class dynamics, recurring behavioural issues, and your child's general development.

Find your child's class teacher in the table below.

Step 3

Cycle Coordinator / School Management — Serious Matters

If a problem cannot be resolved at the class teacher level, or in the case of serious disciplinary matters, the Cycle Coordinator is the next point of contact. In very serious cases, the Deputy Director or the Director will be involved.

Parallel track

DEPS-Referent — Student Well-being & Support

Each class is supported not only by a class teacher, but also by a designated reference person from the DEPS department (ESEB, SePAS, or SSE). This person runs in parallel to the academic escalation path above.

If you have questions related to student support, well-being, learning difficulties, or school organisation, this person is your first point of contact. They can either assist you directly or refer you to the appropriate service.

Find your child's DEPS contact in the class table below.

Student Secretariat

Your first point of contact for illness, early departure, lost cards, and administrative questions. Located at the main entrance.

Email: secretariat.eleves@llis.lu

If your email address changes, please notify the secretariat immediately so you continue to receive all school communications.

SePAS

School Psychological & Social Service — confidential, free support for personal, social, family, or academic difficulties. Each class has a dedicated SePAS contact. No appointment needed for a first visit. Open to students and parents.

SSE — Socio-Educational Service

Organises extracurricular activities, peer mediation, and anti-bullying projects (Coolness Training, S-Team). Supports the broader school community and a positive school climate.

Caretaker (Loge)

For practical issues: lost property, room problems, damaged equipment. Any found object on school premises must be handed in to the Loge as soon as possible. Lost property may be reclaimed from the Loge; uncollected items are disposed of at the end of each term.

School Librarian

For study support, library access, and help finding resources. The library is open Monday–Friday, 08:00–15:00.

Emergency

In an Emergency

European Emergency Number: 112

This number works from any phone, even without credit or a SIM card — police, fire, and ambulance.

  • Fire or evacuation: Students leave calmly via the nearest marked exit. They follow their teacher's instructions, do not use the lift, and assemble at the designated meeting point in the schoolyard.
  • Medical emergency: The teacher alerts the secretariat immediately. Students do not attempt to move an injured person unless there is immediate danger.
  • Security concern (suspicious person, threat): a teacher or staff member is informed immediately. Students do not confront the person themselves.
  • Emergency exits are clearly marked throughout the building. Students are introduced to their nearest exit at the start of the school year.
