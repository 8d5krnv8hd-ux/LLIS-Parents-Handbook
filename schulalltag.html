Accounts

The IAM ID

In August, you will receive a letter by post containing your child's IAM ID and activation code. This ID is the key to every digital service at LLIS.

The IAM ID is generated using the first 3 letters of the child's surname, the first 2 letters of their first name, and 3 random digits — for example: Max Mustermann → MusMa157

To activate the account, go to iam.education.lu and follow the steps in the "IAM account activation" section. You will need: the IAM ID, the activation code, and your child's social security number.

Once activated, the IAM ID gives access to all of the school's digital services with a single login — WebUntis, Teams, mybooks.lu, eduroam WiFi, and more.

WebUntis

Timetables via WebUntis

WebUntis is the school's central platform for timetables, homework, test dates, absences, and class updates. As a parent, it gives you real-time visibility into your child's school life.

Students use WebUntis to view

  • Their class timetable, including any changes
  • Scheduled tests and homework assignments
  • Behavioural notes and comments
  • Content covered in class

Teachers use WebUntis to

  • Record absences and lateness
  • Enter homework, test dates, and behavioural observations
  • Note the learning objectives and content covered in each lesson

Parents can use WebUntis to

  • View your child's timetable, including daily updates and changes
  • Monitor all absences and lateness
  • Track homework, behavioural notes, and upcoming tests
  • See the learning content covered in each lesson
  • Manage information for multiple children at LLIS using a single account

Parent login for WebUntis

As a parent or guardian, you will receive your own login credentials (username and password) for WebUntis, accessible via webuntis.com. You do not need to use your child's account.

At the beginning of October, you will receive personalised login details for parent/tutor access. A step-by-step setup guide is available at: sites.google.com/view/llis-student/untis-mobile ↗

Check it daily

Last-minute room changes and teacher substitutions appear in WebUntis first. Check it every day — especially before leaving home in the morning.

Please do not use your child's Teams account or WebUntis to contact teachers, as this may lead to confusion. Always use email instead — see Contacts.

myCard

Student Card « myCard »

At the beginning of the school year, all LLIS students receive a myCard. This card serves two key purposes.

1. Official Student ID

Issued by the Ministry of Education (MENJE), the myCard is recognised nationwide as the student's official school ID. Students must carry their card on school premises at all times. At the request of any person in authority (management, teaching, administrative and technical staff), the student is obliged to show their card — also for security reasons and to prevent unknown persons from entering the school site.

2. Cashless Payment

The myCard is linked to a secure virtual account for cashless payment at all Restopolis sales points on campus. At checkout, the student taps the card — the amount is automatically debited from the virtual account. A receipt shows the previous and current balance. No money is stored on the card itself.

As a parent, you can follow the money spent by logging into eduguichet.lu or restopolis.lu.

In case of loss or theft

  • Notify the student secretariat immediately — the card will be blocked to prevent misuse.
  • A new card can be issued at the concierge desk (Loge) in the morning only, for a replacement fee of €5.
  • The existing virtual balance is automatically transferred to the new card.

Catering

School Catering / Restopolis

The school canteen account linked to the myCard can be topped up online. To ensure your child can access all canteen services from day one, credit the account before the first school day.

How to top up

Top up online at www.restopolis.lu using a credit card. Instructions are available directly on the Restopolis website. On the first regular school day, each student's myCard will be preloaded with one free meal.

Where to eat

Eating is only allowed in the cafeteria, school restaurant, and atrium. Water is the only beverage permitted in classrooms. Trays and utensils must be returned to the trolleys after use.

Admin

Student Information Form

Each school year, families are asked to complete or update the Student Information Form. This form is how the school keeps your contact details, health information, and authorisations up to date.

  • The form will be sent to you by email — typically in the first communication from the student secretariat at the start of the school year.
  • New students enrolling at LLIS for 2026/2027: please fill in, sign, and return the form.
  • Returning students who completed the form in 2025/2026: please update, sign, and return it only if any information has changed. Otherwise no action is needed.
  • If any information changes during the school year, please contact the student secretariat directly: secretariat.eleves@llis.lu
  • Parents are reminded of their obligation to inform the school immediately of any health issues that may affect their child's school life.

Lockers

Lockers

Lockers are made available to students each school year. A deposit of €20 is required to receive a locker key.

  • Lockers are assigned once classroom locations are confirmed, usually by the end of September. The class teacher will have the allocation list.
  • Returning students who paid the deposit in a previous year do not need to pay again (marked "X" in the deposit list). New students or those who have not yet paid must give €20 in cash to their class teacher.
  • A new deposit is also required if the previously assigned locker was returned in a damaged or unclean condition.
  • Refund: the €20 deposit is refundable only when a student leaves LLIS permanently, and only if the locker is returned clean and in good condition. Download the "Request for Refund" form at llis.lu/downloads and send to cases@llis.lu. Refunds are made by bank transfer.

Photocopies

Photocopies

LLIS collects an annual contribution to cover photocopying and printing costs used for lessons, tests, and educational materials.

  • Students in vocational (Formation professionnelle) and preparatory classes (Voie de préparation) are exempt from photocopying fees, in line with MENJE policy on free schoolbooks.
  • Parents will receive an email at the beginning of October with the photocopying costs for the current school year, along with payment instructions.

Support

Financial Support (Subsidies)

Students whose families are facing financial difficulties may be eligible for state financial support or for assistance from the school's solidarity fund. All requests are handled in complete confidence.

Deadline: 31 October

State financial support applications must be submitted by 31 October. Late applications cannot be accepted. For any information, please contact SePAS directly — see Support & Care.

School Building

Parking & Kiss&Go

To ensure a safe and smooth traffic flow on campus, LLIS follows a clearly structured parking and drop-off system.

Staff Parking — Reserved

The marked parking areas are strictly reserved for LLIS staff during school hours. These zones are not open to parents or students during school hours. After 16:00, the parking is also accessible to external visitors.

Students with driving licences are asked to park at Fréinen.

Kiss & Go — Drop-Off

There are two Kiss & Go lanes: one for Kindergarten + P1–P2 (upper section) and one for P3–P5 + Secondary (lower section).

Drive forward as far as possible → stop briefly → let your child exit → drive away immediately. The central lane is reserved for school buses only.

Exit lane: EXIT ONLY

The lane marked "EXIT ONLY" may be used solely for leaving the site. Entry via this lane is not permitted. This was introduced for safety reasons after several dangerous situations — especially affecting younger primary students.

Consider sustainable alternatives

Many students — especially our older secondary school students — have the option to use public school transportation or travel to school independently by bicycle or on foot. We encourage you to consider these alternatives to reduce morning traffic. If driving is necessary, carpooling is a great way to reduce the number of vehicles on site.

Security

Security & Visiting the School

Any external visitor must check in at reception on the day of their appointment. Unregistered visitors will not be allowed to enter the school premises.

  • Entry to the school is permitted from 07:00. Students may wait in the main hall, cafeteria, or playground before 07:50.
  • Visitors may only attend lessons if they have been explicitly invited by a teacher for pedagogical purposes.

Learning Tools

School Books

Since 2018/19, all students in secondary education and vocational training in Luxembourg are entitled to free schoolbooks.

mybooks.lu

Go to www.mybooks.lu, log in with your child's IAM ID, and select the required textbooks. Then visit a participating bookshop with the app (or a printout of the barcodes) to collect the books — free of charge.

Second-hand books — upbooking.lu

Students who choose second-hand books receive a voucher worth 50% of the value of the unused new books. Find second-hand books and pass on old ones at upbooking.lu.

WiFi

WiFi @ School: eduroam

LLIS uses eduroam — a secure global WiFi network for schools and universities. Once set up, your child can use the same login at thousands of educational institutions worldwide.

  • A step-by-step setup guide is available at: sites.google.com/view/llis-student/eduroam ↗
  • Access to the eduroam WiFi network on school grounds has been restricted to students aged 15 and above — a measure taken by the Ministry based on the concept of digital maturity.

Teams

Teams & Document Sharing

Microsoft Teams is how teachers share documents, assignments, and course materials. Every teacher creates a Teams group for their subject and invites students. Students must check Teams regularly and join the correct groups.

A step-by-step guide to set up Teams on an iPad is available at: sites.google.com/view/llis-student/microsoft-teams ↗

Important: do not contact teachers via Teams or WebUntis

Please do not use your child's Teams account or WebUntis to contact teachers, as this may lead to confusion. To contact teachers, always use email — see Contacts for the email format.

Library

The School Library

The library is open to all students — a quiet space to study, browse, or take a break with a good book. Also available for quiet study during lunchtime.

  • Open Monday–Friday, 08:00–15:00. Reference books and journals available to consult on-site.
  • Students can borrow up to 3 books at a time, for a loan period of one month.
  • If books are overdue, students receive a reminder. Unreturned books may cause the report card to be held back until they are returned.
