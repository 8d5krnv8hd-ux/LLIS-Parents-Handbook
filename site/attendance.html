Attendance

Absences, Illness & Punctuality

What parents need to do when their child is absent, ill, or needs to leave school early — and how unexcused absences are handled.

Absences

Absences

Every absence must be justified by a written excuse submitted to the class teacher. Here is the step-by-step process.

Absences — and especially unexcused absences — are something we take very seriously. As parents and guardians, you are one of our most important partners in this process. The procedures below are designed to keep you fully informed.

Please note: the automatic end-of-day absence notification email is currently not active for technical reasons. We are working on reintroducing an improved version and will inform you in advance when it is ready.

DAY 1

Inform the school (absence signalée)

If a student is absent, parents/guardians (or students over 18) must inform the school as early as possible by email: secretariat.eleves@llis.lu or phone: +352 276963-220. The Student Secretariat is open from 07:15.

Once the school has been informed, the absence is recorded in WebUntis as a notified absence.

Please note

Informing the school does not automatically excuse the absence. A written explanation must be submitted to the class teacher. See the next step.

3 Days or less

Absences of up to 3 days

If the absence is due to illness or force majeure, parents (or adult students) must inform the class teacher in writing within three calendar days, explaining the reason for the absence (→ excuse form).

If a valid explanation is provided within three days, the absence will be excused.
If no explanation is provided within this period, the absence will be considered unexcused.

⚠ The deadline in concomitant classes (vocational training) is eight calendar days.

4+ Days or more

If an absence exceeds three consecutive school days, a medical certificate is required.

Appointments that can be scheduled outside school hours must be arranged accordingly.

Students are asked to schedule medical and dental appointments outside of school hours. In exceptional cases and upon special request, the class teacher may exempt the student from attending lessons for this reason.

Medical Certificate

Medical Certificate

A medical certificate may be required to officially excuse an absence. Here is when one is needed.

  • After an absence of more than three consecutive school days.
  • If the director or class teacher considers it necessary, they may require a medical certificate for every absence.
  • From S4 to S7: a medical certificate is required for any absence during a scheduled B-test.

Illness

If Your Child Is Ill at School

A student who becomes ill during the school day must go directly to the Student Secretariat and must not leave the building alone.

  • If your child feels unwell during the school day, they tell their teacher and go directly to the Student Secretariat. They must not simply leave.
  • For minor students (under 18), the secretariat will contact parents or legal guardians before authorising departure.
  • Any unauthorised departure will result in disciplinary action.
  • Students on a vocational training programme: the secretariat will also contact the employer if early departure is needed.

Do not arrange pick-up before checking with the secretariat

Even if your child contacts you directly from school to say they feel unwell, the official process must be followed — your child goes to the secretariat, the secretariat contacts you. This protects your child and ensures the school knows where they are at all times.

Planned Absences

Planning an Absence in Advance

For foreseeable absences, a prior written request must be submitted well in advance. The earlier you notify the school, the easier it is for your child to prepare.

  • For any foreseeable absence of one day (sports competition, funeral abroad, etc.), a written request with clear and precise reasons must be sent to the class teacher at least three days in advance.
  • For an absence of one day immediately before the start of the holidays or after the resumption of classes, or for any absence exceeding one day, approval from the Headmaster is required.
  • Students are asked to schedule medical and dental appointments outside of school hours. In exceptional cases and upon special request, the class teacher may grant an exemption.
  • Driving test (practical exam only): Students wishing to be exempted to pass their driving test must submit a request with a copy of the letter of invitation to the class teacher, who may grant special leave. Exemptions for practical driving lessons or for training at the centre in Colmar-Berg will not be granted.
  • Religious holidays (e.g. Eid al-Fitr, Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, and others after checking with school management): the absence will be accepted. Your child is responsible for catching up on all missed work.

Please note

Please send these requests as early as possible, as responses may take some time and cannot always be provided immediately.

Tests & Assessments

Retaking an Assignment / Catch-Up Test

A student absent on the day of a test must arrange a catch-up session with the subject teacher as soon as possible.

  • If your child is absent on the day of a test, they must contact the subject teacher as soon as possible to request a catch-up session.
  • The Student Secretariat will send an email to the parents inviting the student to the catch-up session fixed by the subject teacher.
  • If a student is absent again on the day of the catch-up test, a medical certificate is required. Without one, the test may be considered as failed.

Please note

Students who need to retake a test may exceptionally have more than one test on the same day.

S4–S7: B-Tests

For students in S4–S7, a medical certificate is required for every absence on the day of a B-test. The Student Secretariat will contact parents immediately if a student is absent for a B-test.

Punctuality

Punctuality

Students must be in their classroom and ready to work at the time indicated in their timetable. Repeated unjustified lateness is treated as unexcused absences.

  • Students must be present when the lesson begins. Arriving late disrupts the class and is disrespectful to the teacher and classmates.
  • At the first bell (07:55), students go directly to their classroom and wait quietly.
  • A student who arrives late must apologise to the teacher upon entry — the teacher may not refuse admission.
  • Repeated, unjustified lateness will be recorded as unexcused absences by the class teacher.
  • If your child is late due to a public transport delay, they should report to the secretariat and mention the bus line affected.
  • If the teacher has not arrived 10 minutes after the lesson start, the class representative informs the Student Secretariat.

Leaving School

Leaving School Grounds

During lesson times and small breaks, students may not leave school premises without a justified reason approved by the school.

  • Students are not permitted to leave the LLIS premises during lesson times or small breaks without a justified reason approved by the school (illness, etc.).
  • Students are not permitted to leave the LLIS premises during school hours without prior written approval from the Student Secretariat.
  • In case of illness or force majeure, a student may leave only with written authorisation from the secretariat — see Ill at School above.
  • The class teacher is informed automatically whenever a student leaves early.
  • Any unauthorised departure will be treated as a serious violation of school rules and will result in disciplinary measures.

Lunch break

During the lunch break, students may leave the school grounds.

Escalation

Unexcused Absences — Escalation Steps

Unjustified absences are treated as unexcused and may result in disciplinary action. The school is required to escalate in steps.

ThresholdWhat happens
20 lessons The class teacher contacts the parents (usually by email or phone) and simultaneously informs the SePAS representative, the relevant Cycle Coordinator, and the Student Secretariat. The school sends an official written warning letter to the parents. The SePAS representative invites the student for a meeting.
48 lessons
(compulsory schooling only)		 The class teacher informs the Cycle Coordinator and the SePAS representative. The Cycle Coordinator then contacts the Ministry of Education. The Ministry sends a formal warning to the parents and informs the Student Secretariat.
49 lessons
(compulsory schooling only)		 The class teacher informs the Cycle Coordinator again and the Ministry is notified once more. At this stage, the Ministry may refer the case to the juvenile court.
60 lessons The class teacher informs the Cycle Coordinator and SePAS. Together, they discuss whether to propose a Disciplinary Council. A recommendation is made to the Director and/or Deputy Director. Under art. 43 of the amended law of 25 June 2004, the Disciplinary Council can pronounce expulsion for more than 60 unjustified absences during the same school year (or more than 30 for students in concomitant classes). The SePAS representative works closely with the student and the parents to help prevent school dropout.

This almost never happens when parents stay in contact

If something is making it difficult for your child to attend school, please reach out early — to the class teacher, SePAS, or school management. They are there to support you and your child. See Support & Care for an overview of available services.
