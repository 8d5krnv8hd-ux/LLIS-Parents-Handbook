Behaviour & School Rules

One Source of Truth

Why we don't duplicate the rules here

LLIS's rules and behaviour expectations are fully documented in the Student Handbook. Duplicating them in the Parents Handbook would mean two versions to maintain — and sooner or later, they would drift apart.

Instead, this page explains what exists and links directly to the relevant sections. When rules are updated during the school year, the Student Handbook is updated — and the links below will always point to the current version.

For parents and students: read it together

The Student Handbook is written for students, but it is equally useful for parents. Reading it together gives you a shared reference point — and makes it easier to discuss expectations at home. If your child has a question about a rule, the Student Handbook is the first place to look.

Disciplinary Council

The Disciplinary Process

For serious or repeated violations, LLIS has a formal disciplinary process. Parents are always informed and involved before any formal measure is taken.

  • Minor issues are handled by the class teacher or school management through informal conversations, written warnings, or detention.
  • More serious or repeated violations may result in a formal warning recorded in the student's file, or a temporary exclusion from school.
  • The Disciplinary Council is convened for the most serious cases. Parents are invited to attend and to speak on behalf of their child. The student is also heard.
  • Possible outcomes include a final written warning, temporary exclusion, or — in extreme cases — permanent exclusion from LLIS.
  • All decisions can be appealed through the Ministry of Education within a set timeframe.

If your child is facing a disciplinary matter

Contact the class teacher or school management as soon as possible. You are entitled to know what the accusation is, to see the relevant evidence, and to be present at any formal hearing. SePAS can also provide support — see Support & Care.

Mobile Phones

Mobile Phones at LLIS

The full mobile phone policy is in the Rules A–Z. Here is a brief summary of what parents most often ask about.

  • Mobile phones are generally not permitted for use during lessons unless a teacher explicitly authorises it for a specific educational activity.
  • During breaks and lunchtime, the rules depend on the student's class year — check the full policy in the Student Handbook for the specific rules applicable to your child's year group.
  • A phone confiscated during a lesson is held by the teacher until end of day. Repeated violations lead to escalating consequences.
  • Photography and video recording of staff, students, or school premises without consent is prohibited and may lead to disciplinary action.

Dress Code

Dress Code

LLIS does not have a uniform, but it does have a dress code. Students are expected to dress appropriately for an educational setting.

The dress code is detailed in the Rules A–Z in the Student Handbook. The general expectation is that clothing is clean, tidy, and appropriate for school — clothing that is offensive, disruptive, or overly revealing is not permitted.

For physical education, students need appropriate sports clothes and shoes. These should be kept in their locker or bag — changing into sports clothes happens at school.

Open Door

Questions or concerns? Talk to the school.

Rules work best when they are understood, not just enforced. If you or your child have questions about any rule or decision, the school is open to a conversation.

Class teacher — first contact

For most questions about your child's behaviour or a specific situation in class, the class teacher is the right first contact. They know your child best and can often resolve things quickly.
