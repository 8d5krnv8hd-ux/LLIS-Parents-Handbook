Support & Care

Support & Care

LLIS has a range of support services for students. This page gives parents an overview of who does what — so you know where to turn when your child needs a little extra help.

SePAS

SePAS — School Psychological & Social Service

SePAS offers free, confidential support for any personal, social, family, or academic difficulty. It is open to students and — where relevant — parents too.

What SePAS can help with

  • Personal difficulties: stress, anxiety, motivation, self-confidence
  • Social difficulties: friendships, conflict with classmates, feeling isolated
  • Family situations affecting school performance
  • Academic difficulties and learning differences
  • Orientation and career guidance questions

How it works

Each class has a dedicated SePAS contact. No appointment is needed for a first visit — students can drop in. All conversations are confidential. SePAS works closely with class teachers, school management, and external services where needed.

SePAS is also a resource for parents. If you are worried about your child but not sure who to talk to, SePAS is a good starting point.

For students: you do not need a reason to go

Your child does not need to have a "serious" problem to visit SePAS. A bad week, a tricky friendship situation, or just wanting to talk to someone outside their immediate circle is enough. Encourage your child to know where SePAS is and that the door is open.

SSE

SSE — Socio-Educational Service

The SSE team organises activities and programmes that build a positive school community — both inside and outside the classroom.

  • Extracurricular activities: sports, clubs, cultural events and workshops organised throughout the school year. The activity list is published at the start of term.
  • Coolness Training: a structured group programme that teaches students how to handle conflict constructively, without aggression. Runs at class level throughout the year.
  • S-Team: a peer-led anti-bullying team of trained older students who mediate and support their classmates. The S-Team is visible at school and approachable.
  • Peer mediation: if your child is in a conflict with another student, SSE can facilitate a structured conversation to find a solution both sides can live with.

Klass+

Klass+

Klass+ is for students who are finding it difficult to follow the normal school routine — not necessarily because of academic struggles, but because of how they experience the school day.

Klass+ provides individual, tailored support for students who need a slightly different structure. This might be a student who feels overwhelmed by the pace of school, a student returning from a long absence, or one who is dealing with behavioural or motivational difficulties that are getting in the way of learning.

Klass+ works alongside the class teacher, SePAS, and if needed, the family. Referral to Klass+ is initiated by the class teacher or school management — parents are always informed and involved.

Klass+ is not a disciplinary measure

Klass+ is a support service, not a punishment. Students who use it continue in their class; Klass+ simply provides a structured space and additional support on top of their normal school day.

Homework

Homework Support

LLIS offers supervised homework support five days a week. A teacher is on hand to assist — students can come for help with a specific subject or simply for a quiet place to work.

Times

Monday–Friday, 14:00–15:20. Students may arrive at the start and leave at the end, or stay for the full duration — their choice.

What happens

A teacher supervises and is available for questions. Students work on homework, test preparation, or catch-up work. The session is not a tutoring course — it is supervised study with expert support available.

Étude Surveillée

Étude Surveillée — Supervised Study

A structured, teacher-supervised study session offered as an alternative to the after-school homework slot. Places are limited and must be registered in advance.

  • The étude is available for core subjects. A supervising teacher provides assistance and ensures a focused working environment.
  • Registration must be completed by the end of September. Speak to the class teacher or secretariat to register.
  • Students who register are expected to attend consistently — irregular attendance may result in their place being offered to another student.

Lunchtime

Lunchtime Spaces

Students have a range of spaces available during the lunch break — for eating, relaxing, studying, or talking to a staff member.

  • School restaurant (Restopolis): hot meals available during both lunch periods — Period 6 (12:00–12:45) and Period 7 (12:50–13:35).
  • Atrium (ground floor): students may eat lunch here during breaks and lunchtime. Food from the cafeteria may be brought there.
  • Library: open 08:00–15:00 — available as a quiet study or reading space at lunchtime.
  • SePAS room: students can drop in at lunchtime to talk — no appointment needed.

Note: corridors are normally off-limits before 07:50 and during breaks. Students may always access their own locker individually, but should not linger in the corridors.
